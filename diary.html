Diary

Daily narrative of our Kashmir adventure

Day 1

Arrival & First Impressions

Arrival in Kashmir and Shikara Experience

"We hopped off the overnight train in Jammu in the freezing morning. Our bus was meant to carry us to Kashmir, but the driver insisted we take the train instead due to heavy road congestion. So we hopped into a local service — a rich, raw ride alongside Kashmiris."

The view from the window was pure magic: barren trees, snow-capped peaks, small villages resting under a purple-orange sky. Meanwhile, our bags were safely routed by bus.

As we entered Srinagar at sunset, we rushed straight to Dal Lake for a Shikara ride. The freezing water flowed quietly beneath us, reflecting purple clouds and distant mountains. It wasn't the best time to do it, the light was dim, and the cold was piercing, but we fell in love with Kashmir's soul in that moment.

Day 2

Adventure & Exploration

Haleema Quest and Gulmarg Slopes

"We rose before dawn, walked through freezing Srinagar's labyrinthine streets in search of Haleema. The food itself wasn't extraordinary — not what the reels made it seem — but the walk, the silence, the feeling of sharing something intimate and pure made it worth it."

Later we rushed back, hopped into our bus by 11–12 and headed toward Gulmarg. The ride upward was stunning, forests blended into barren slopes. At the base, we hopped into the gondola to phase 1.

There wasn't much snow this time, the slopes were patchier than last year, but it was freezing, and we were much more prepared this time. Walking in rental boots, we made the most of it, clicking photos, laughing, and freezing together.

Day 3

Peace & Departure

Sunrise Shikara, Sightseeing and Departure

"For this, we made sure we were up by 6 AM. The Haleema shop was quieter, more intimate. After a warm breakfast, we hopped into a Shikara again — this time for a peaceful, soul-stirring ride during the first rays of the day."

The lake was mistier, more dramatic, and we felt a deep peace we hadn't tasted all trip.

Later, we walked through Chashma Shahi, Nishat Bagh, and Jamia Masjid, and boarded a 9-hour journey back to Delhi by evening — marking the end of a wonderful, freezing, food-and-fun filled journey.

Journey's End

Three days of friendship, adventure, and unforgettable memories in the heart of Kashmir.

From freezing mornings to sunset Shikara rides, every moment was a testament to the bond we share as Othirakadakam.
